Obesity and Income Analysis by State in the U.S.

Correlations Discovered Between Obesity and Income

The chart above examines the relationship between poverty rates and obesity. The data set used for this analysis was collected by the United States Census Bureau, specifically the 2014 ACS 1-year estimates. The data set includes rates of income, obesity, poverty, etc. aggregated by state. This scatterplot visualizes poverty rates and obesity showing a slight positive correlation. Some outliers of the data include Colorado and Washington, DC. While their poverty rates are fairly high, their obesity rates remained low. Inversely, North Dakota had a poverty rate lower than Colorado but a fairly high obesity rate. To explore this graph on your own, hover over each circle to view the exact poverty and obesity rates for each state.
